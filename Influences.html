My Chess Influences

Grandmasters

Some grandmaster who I consider influential to me:

  • Bobby Fischer: First grandmaster and world champion I was introduced to after watching this game in the U.S championship
  • Garry Kasparov: I watched a lot of his talks and followed some of his games on youtube.
  • Anatoly Karpov I followed some annotated games on youtube and also viewed a few of his courses. One of the earliest was his game against Yusupov
  • Mikhail Tal: Amazing Chess player. His games and style was truly genius, imaginative and tactical. I followed his game by viewing Mato Jelic's chess channel
  • Emanuel Lasker: I read his chess manuel and he comes off as extremely intelligent. He also teaches chess players to use their own intellect and think independently.
  • Richard Reti: His books with anotated games are just beautiful to follow along with. I would go through his books with a relaxing and warm cup of coffee!
  • Alexandra Kotov: The first player to introduce me to the three fishes of chess mastery:
    1. Positional Play
    2. Tactical vision
    3. Calculation!
  • Roman dzindzichashvili:Taught me everything I know about openings!
  • Max Euwe:Briefly read his book on strategy and planning
  • Alexandra Alekhine:A fantastic tactician. I went through a database of many of his games
  • Capablanca:My idol! First positional masterpiece I saw of him is this video

    Books

    Here are some of the books that have shaped my understanding of chess:

    • "My System" by Aron Nimzowitsch: A classic on positional play and strategy.
    • "Silman's Complete Endgame Course" by Jeremy Silman: A comprehensive guide to endgames.
    • "The Art of Attack in Chess" by Vladimir Vuković: A must-read for attacking players.
    • "Think Like a Grandmaster" by Alexander Kotov: Focuses on calculation and decision-making.
    • "Play Like a Grandmaster" by Alexander Kotov
    • "How to Reassess Your Chess" by Jeremy Silman
    • "Masters of the Chess Board" by Richard Réti
    • "Lasker's Chess Manual" by Emanuel Lasker
    • "Yusupov's Chess Fundamentals" by Artur Yusupov
    • "Fred Reinfeld's Complete Chess Course" by Fred Reinfeld
    • "Can You Be a Tactical Genius?" by Angus Dunnington
    • "The Complete Book of Chess Strategy" by Jeremy Silman
    • "Bobby Fischer Teaches Chess" by Bobby Fischer: A great book for learning chess tactics and strategies.
    • "My 60 Memorable Games" by Bobby Fischer: A collection of Fischer's most famous games with his annotations.
